TYPE 0

  • Do it now or you will never be successful.

  • Hurry up or you will miss the bus.

  • If you heat ice, it melts.

TYPE 1

    S + Vs/es, S + will/can/may + V

  • If you go out without approriate reasons, you will be fine 2m VND.
  • If you catch coronavirus, you may die.

TYPE 2

    S + Ved/2, S + would/could/might + V

  • If COVID were away, many people would go shopping. (Now we have COVID everywhere)
  • If you had a lot of money, what would you do? (Now you have little money)

TYPE 3

    S + had + Ved/3, S + would/could/might + have + Ved/3

    Note: Ved/3 = P.P (Past participle). So we could rewrite as:

    --> S + had + P.P, S + would/could/might + have + P.P

MIXED

Mixed usually means 2 types in a sentence.


Here is an example: "John stayed up late watching EURO matches. He is tired now."

We could say: If John hadn't stayed up late watching EURO matches, he would not be tired


I know it's not clear to you yet. I will give you an another example:

Nam went to the market yesterday. He is now positive with the coronavirus.

We could say: If Nam hadn't gone to the market yesterday, he would not be now positive with the coronavirus.

IN GENERAL

Usually there are two kinds of questions related to conditionals.

  1. You will see IF in your question, you need to complete one side of the if statement.

    2. Example: If you heat ice, it _______.

    A. melt B. melts C. would melt D. melting

  2. You need to rewrite using conditionals from a given situation.

    Example: Nam is not here. The meeting is not fun.

    You will have four options, but the exact answer could be: If Nam were here, the meeting would be fun.

OK. It's enough. One last rule: if you meet the second situation above, NOT will be removed and vice versea (ngược lại). Look at the examples again, you will understand what I mean.
